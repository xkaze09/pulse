Telco Store Case Management Process

Actors:
- Customer
- Store Frontliner (Retail Associate)
- Store Supervisor
- Backend Support Team
- Case Management Team

Process Flow:

1. Customer enters the store and reports an issue, complaint, or service request to the Store Frontliner.

2. Store Frontliner greets the customer, listens to the issue, and records the details in the system.

3. Store Frontliner assesses the issue: Can it be resolved on-the-spot?
   - If YES (solvable at store level, e.g. SIM swap, basic account reset, plan change): Frontliner resolves the issue immediately.
     - Customer confirms satisfaction. Process ends successfully.
   - If NO (requires backend investigation, billing dispute, network issue): Continue to create a case.

4. Store Frontliner creates a case ticket in the CRM system, recording all issue details, customer information, and priority level.

5. Store Supervisor evaluates the case:
   - Does it require escalation to the Backend Support Team?
   - If YES: Case is escalated to the appropriate Backend Support Team (Billing, Technical, or Provisioning).
   - If NO: Supervisor handles the case resolution directly.

6. Backend Support Team receives the escalated case, performs root-cause analysis, and works on a resolution (network diagnostics, billing correction, account provisioning).

7. Case Management Team monitors all open cases to ensure SLA compliance and timely updates. They flag cases approaching SLA breach.

8. Backend Support Team proposes a resolution and sends an automated notification to the customer via SMS or email.

9. Customer reviews the proposed resolution:
   - If the customer ACCEPTS: Customer confirms satisfaction. Store Frontliner closes the case in CRM and completes closure documentation. Process ends successfully.
   - If the customer REJECTS: Case is reopened and re-escalated to the Backend Support Team for further investigation. Return to step 6.

10. Store Frontliner documents the case closure with full resolution details in the CRM system.
